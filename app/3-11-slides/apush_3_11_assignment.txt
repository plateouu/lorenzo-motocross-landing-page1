1. DETAILED LESSON OUTLINE
---------------------------------------------------------
Title of Lesson: The New Negro Movement & The Harlem Renaissance
Topic: AP African American Studies 3.11

EKs and LOs:
- LO 3.11.A: Describe ways the New Negro movement emphasized self-definition, racial pride, and cultural innovation.
- EK 3.11.A.1: The New Negro movement encouraged African Americans to define their own identity and to advocate for themselves politically in the midst of the nadir's atrocities.
- EK 3.11.A.2: The New Negro movement pursued the creation of a Black aesthetic, which was reflected in the artistic and cultural achievements of Black creators.
- EK 3.11.A.3: The New Negro movement produced innovations in music, art, and literature that served as counternarratives to prevailing racial stereotypes. These artistic innovations reflected the migrations of African Americans from the South to urban centers.
- EK 3.11.A.4: The movement encompassed several cultural movements, including the Harlem Renaissance.

Summary of Lecture Content:
- What Was the New Negro Movement?: A movement challenging the inferiority narrative. Encouraged Black Americans to define their own identity and advocate politically. Alain Locke redefined it as an aesthetic movement in 1925.
- A Black Aesthetic: Pursuit of distinct art rooted in Black experiences. Writers like Langston Hughes and Zora Neale Hurston celebrated Black life. Locke encouraged rejecting the burden of representing the entire race.
- Innovations in Art & Music: Blues and jazz (Armstrong, Ellington) served as counternarratives to stereotypes. Art by Aaron Douglas blended African imagery with modern styles. Connected directly to the Great Migration.
- The Harlem Renaissance: The cultural revolution of the 1920s-30s. Harlem became the cultural capital, giving pride and showcasing the richness of Black culture.
- Lasting Impact: Overturned inferiority narratives, formed the foundation of modern American music, and laid the community groundwork for the Civil Rights Movement.

Description of Activity: "Debate It Out" using Primary Sources (15 mins)
- Students use historical evidence from Alain Locke's "The New Negro" and Langston Hughes's "The Negro Artist and the Racial Mountain" to construct arguments.
- Students prepare for 3 minutes and engage in a 5-minute structured debate against their peers.
- Debate Topics (randomly assigned): 
  1. "Was the Black Aesthetic or Political Advocacy more important?"
  2. "Who had a greater impact: Locke or Hughes?" 
  3. "What is the greatest legacy of the Harlem Renaissance?"

3. ACED SAQs FROM GUIDED NOTES
---------------------------------------------------------
Prompt 1: Briefly explain ONE way the Great Migration influenced the cultural innovations of the New Negro movement.
A (Answer): The Great Migration brought millions of African Americans from the rural South to urban Northern centers, bringing their localized cultural traditions with them.
C (Cite): As Southern Black populations migrated to cities like Harlem, they brought musical traditions such as the blues.
E (Explain): In these urban centers, these distinct Southern traditions evolved and fused into new genres like jazz. This mass gathering of Black individuals in cities allowed for a flourishing of concentrated cultural exchange that birthed the Harlem Renaissance.

Prompt 2: Briefly explain ONE goal of the "Black Aesthetic" as defined during the Harlem Renaissance.
A (Answer): One primary goal of the Black Aesthetic was to create a counternarrative against prevailing racist stereotypes by celebrating authentic Black life.
C (Cite): Thinkers like Alain Locke and Langston Hughes urged Black artists to embrace their own racial identity and cultural traditions rather than seeking white approval or imitating white standards.
E (Explain): By focusing on their own unique experiences, rhythms, and beauty—and rejecting the burden of having to "perfectly" represent the entire race to white audiences—Black artists reclaimed their self-definition and fostered deep racial pride within their communities.

4. SOURCE IDENTIFICATION & EXPLANATION
---------------------------------------------------------
Source 1:
- Title: The New Negro
- Author/Origin: Alain Locke
- Date: 1925
- Type: Essay/Anthology
- Primary or secondary: Primary
- Historical context: Published during the height of the Harlem Renaissance, following the Great Migration and amidst the strict segregation and racial violence of the Nadir of race relations.
- Explanation of why the source is important: Locke’s work is the foundational text of the movement. It articulated the "new psychology" of African Americans—a mindset that rejected subservience and fully embraced cultural self-expression and racial pride. It provided the intellectual framework for the Renaissance.

Source 2:
- Title: The Negro Artist and the Racial Mountain
- Author/Origin: Langston Hughes
- Date: 1926
- Type: Essay
- Primary or secondary: Primary
- Historical context: Written during the Harlem Renaissance as a response to Black writers who wished to be viewed simply as "poets" rather than "Negro poets," which Hughes interpreted as a desire to be white.
- Explanation of why the source is important: This essay serves as a powerful manifesto for the Black Aesthetic. It explicitly argues that Black artists must draw upon their own rich cultural heritage without shame or fear, marking a definitive shift toward unapologetic self-definition in African American art.

5. MCQ ANSWER KEY WITH EXPLANATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------
Question 1: The New Negro movement most directly reflected which of the following developments in the early twentieth century?
A. The decline of African American political activism after Reconstruction
B. Federal legislation that protected the civil rights of African Americans in northern cities
C. A growing emphasis on self-definition, racial pride, and cultural expression among African Americans
D. The integration of African American artists into mainstream white cultural institutions

Correct Answer: C
Explanation: 
- Why C is correct: The New Negro movement was defined by its push for self-definition, racial pride, and cultural independence.
- Why distractors are incorrect: A is incorrect as political activism transformed and grew (NAACP, UNIA). B is incorrect as there was no widespread federal legislation protecting civil rights in Northern cities. D is incorrect as the movement sought independent Black cultural expression, not integration into white institutions.

Question 2: Langston Hughes's essay "The Negro Artist and the Racial Mountain" most directly argues for which of the following?
A. African American artists should seek approval from white critics to gain wider recognition.
B. African American literature should focus exclusively on the experience of slavery.
C. The Harlem Renaissance was primarily a political movement rather than an artistic one.
D. Black artists should embrace their own racial identity and cultural traditions in their work.

Correct Answer: D
Explanation:
- Why D is correct: Hughes powerfully argued that Black artists must proudly embrace their own culture, rhythm, and life without fear or shame.
- Why distractors are incorrect: A is incorrect; he actively criticized Black artists who catered to white audiences. B is incorrect because he celebrated contemporary, everyday Black life. C is incorrect as the essay focused explicitly on the role of the artist and cultural production.

Question 3: Which of the following best describes the relationship between the Great Migration and the Harlem Renaissance?
A. The migration of African Americans from the South brought cultural traditions that helped fuel artistic innovations in urban centers like Harlem.
B. The Great Migration had no significant impact on cultural developments in northern cities.
C. The Harlem Renaissance primarily drew on European artistic traditions rather than African American culture.
D. The Great Migration was caused by the success of the Harlem Renaissance in attracting Southern Black workers.

Correct Answer: A
Explanation:
- Why A is correct: Migrants brought southern cultural traditions (like blues) that evolved and blossomed in northern cities, directly fueling the Renaissance.
- Why distractors are incorrect: B is incorrect; the influx transformed urban culture. C is incorrect; the Renaissance relied on distinctively African American roots. D is incorrect; economic opportunities and escaping Jim Crow caused the Migration, which then enabled the Renaissance.
