INTRODUCTION (THE CORE IDEA)
"My project is about how social media apps like TikTok and Instagram control what we see, and how that pressure affects the people who make content. These apps use 'algorithms' (hidden rules) to decide if a video goes viral or stays hidden. I want to know how this system stresses out new creators and forces them to change who they are just to get views."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART-BY-PART BREAKDOWN:

1. THE PROBLEM
The main issue is that new creators feel like they HAVE to follow trends to be successful. If they don't do what's popular, the app hides their work. This makes people stop being themselves and causes a lot of stress.

2. RESEARCH QUESTION
I am asking: "How does the pressure to get more views actually change how new creators feel and what they decide to post?"

3. CONCEPTS
I'm looking at two things: The **Algorithm** (the app's computer brain that picks winners) and **Small Creators** (regular people just starting out).

4. PREDICTIONS
I predict that the more someone cares about views, the more likely they are to 'act fake' or just copy whatever is trending. This pressure probably leads to higher stress levels.

5. VALUE OF STUDY
This matters because it explains why so much content on social media looks the same. It helps us understand the mental health cost of trying to 'beat the system' just to be seen.

6. THE METHOD (How I'm Researching)
I am using two main tools to find the truth:
- **Anonymous Surveys:** I'll send these to hundreds of creators to get 'the numbers' on how many people feel stressed.
- **Interviews:** I'll talk to 10 creators for 10 minutes each to hear their personal stories.

7. THEMATIC CODING (What I'm Looking For)
After the interviews, I will use **Thematic Coding**. This just means I'm going to read through everything they said and look for 'themes' (patterns). For example, if 8 out of 10 people say 'I felt like I had to delete my art because it didn't get views,' that's a pattern. I'm looking for clear proof that view counts and mental stress are linked.

8. THE GAP
Most experts study big influencers. I am focusing on the 'First Steps'—the moment a regular person starts an account and first feels the pressure of the algorithm.

9. EXPERT SOURCES & EVIDENCE
I'm using books by experts who explain how apps 'hide' people (Digital Invisibility). I'll combine their theories with my own survey and interview results.

10. ETHICS & SAFETY
I am keeping everyone anonymous. No names will be used so people can be 100% honest about their stress.

11. NEXT STEPS & PROBLEMS
I'm finishing my reading now. Next, I’ll find creators on Reddit and Discord to interview. The hardest part is finding 'invisible' creators—the ones the algorithm is already hiding!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY CONNECTION:
"Basically, I'm connecting the math (the algorithm) to the human (the creator). I'm trying to prove that the way these apps are built is forcing us to be less authentic and more stressed."
