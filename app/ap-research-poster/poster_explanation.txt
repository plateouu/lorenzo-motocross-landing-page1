INTRODUCTION (THE HOOK)
"Imagine you are a brand-new artist. You spend hours creating something original, but when you post it, only 3 people see it. Meanwhile, a 15-second video of someone dancing to a trending song gets 2 million views. This is the reality of social media algorithms—the hidden codes that act as digital gatekeepers, deciding who gets heard and who stays invisible."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART-BY-PART BREAKDOWN & CONNECTIONS:

1. THE PROBLEM (The 'Why' It Matters)
Connection: This sets the stage. It explains that creators aren't just 'sad' about views; they are being forced into a 'trend-or-fail' cycle. This cycle creates a psychological trap where authenticity is sacrificed for visibility.

2. RESEARCH QUESTION (The Core Focus)
Connection: This is the bridge between the problem and the study. It narrows the focus to two variables: how creators FEEL (internal psychology) and what they POST (external choice).

3. CONCEPTS (Defining the Terms)
Connection: To understand the study, we must agree on what an 'Algorithm' is (the gatekeeper) and who 'Small Creators' are (the subjects). It keeps the research grounded in specific digital mechanics.

4. PREDICTIONS (The Hypothesis)
Connection: Based on the problem, I predict that 'acting fake' isn't just a choice—it’s a survival tactic. I assume the pressure to be viral directly causes stress and content homogenization (everyone posting the same thing).

5. VALUE OF STUDY (The Bigger Picture)
Connection: This explains the 'So What?' if new creators are stressed, our entire digital culture becomes a copy-paste mirror. It connects individual stress to a larger societal issue of toxic online behavior.

6. THE METHOD (The Strategy)
Connection: I am using a 'Mixed Methods' approach. The survey gives me the numbers (the breadth), and the interviews give me the stories (the depth). They work together to prove the psychological impact.

7. RATIONALE (Why This Way?)
Connection: Numbers (surveys) alone can't show emotion. I need to hear the 'exact moments' of stress in an interview to truly answer the 'How' in my research question.

8. THE GAP (The Missing Piece)
Connection: Many people study how algorithms affect big celebrities or 'influencers.' My research specifically targets the 'New Account'—the most vulnerable stage of the creator journey that others have ignored.

9. EXPERT SOURCES (The Foundation)
Connection: I’m building on the work of Gillespie (2014) and Bucher (2012). They provided the theory of 'algorithmic visibility,' which I am now applying to the specific psychology of small creators.

10. OWN EVIDENCE (The Proof)
Connection: This is where my personal work comes in. The survey results and interview transcripts provide the raw data that will either prove or disprove my predictions.

11. SAFETY & RIGHTS (The Ethics)
Connection: Because I am asking about mental health and stress, I must protect my participants. Anonymity is key to getting honest answers about 'faking it' for the algorithm.

12. NEXT STEPS (The Roadmap)
Connection: This shows where I am now vs. where I’m going. It transitions the project from planning to active data collection.

13. WHAT'S MISSING & POTENTIAL PROBLEMS (The Reality Check)
Connection: No study is perfect. I acknowledge that subscription-based apps (like Patreon) might be the 'antidote' to algorithmic stress, and I admit that finding 'invisible' creators is a challenge I must overcome.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FINAL SUMMARY CONNECTION:
"From the Hook to the Next Steps, every part of this poster is connected by one theme: The Human Cost of the Code. We are investigating how a hidden math formula changes the way humans express themselves."
