ELEVATOR PITCH (2-MINUTE VERSION) - TAYLOR DAAN

"Hi everyone! My research focuses on the hidden gatekeepers of our digital lives: social media algorithms.

Think about how apps like TikTok and Instagram decide which videos you see every day. They use a secret code to pick the 'winners' and 'losers.' If you're a big celebrity, you're fine. But for new, small creators who are just starting out, this creates a huge amount of psychological pressure.

The main problem is that if you don't follow the latest trend, nobody sees your work. New creators feel forced to copy whatever is already famous just to get a few views. This leads to massive stress and an identity crisis for young artists. They stop being authentic and start 'acting fake' just to satisfy the code.

This led me to my core research question: How does the constant pressure to get views actually change how new creators feel about themselves and what they choose to post?

To answer this, I’m doing two things. First, an anonymous survey to see how many people feel this algorithmic anxiety. Second, I’m doing 10-minute interviews to hear their real stories. I want to hear about the exact moment someone felt like they had to change their personal style just to avoid being 'invisible' online.

Experts in this field have already shown that algorithms make us anxious. But my study is unique because I am looking specifically at brand-new accounts right when they are first trying to build an audience.

This matters because it explains why our online communities can feel toxic or unauthentic sometimes. It helps us understand why everyone seems to be copying each other instead of sharing original ideas. By seeing the human cost of these algorithms, we can fix how we talk to each other in digital spaces.

The biggest challenge? The algorithm hides the very people I need to interview! But my next steps are to finish my expert reading and start finding those first 10 creators in online forums and subreddits. Thank you."
