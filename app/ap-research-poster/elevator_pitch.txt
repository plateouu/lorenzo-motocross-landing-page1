ELEVATOR PITCH (SIMPLE VERSION) - TAYLOR DAAN
----------------------------------------

"Hey everyone! My research is about how social media apps like TikTok and Instagram use hidden code—called algorithms—to pick which videos go viral.

For new creators, this creates a lot of pressure. If you don't follow the latest trend, nobody sees your work. This makes people stressed, and it forces them to act 'fake' or change their own style just to get views.

My big question is: How does this pressure to get views actually change the mental health and the choices of new creators?

To find out, I’m doing an anonymous survey to see how many people feel this way. Then, I’m doing 10-minute interviews to hear their real stories. I want to see the exact moment someone felt forced to change their style because of the algorithm.

This study matters because it explains why so much of social media feels like everyone is just copying each other. It helps us understand why online communities can feel toxic or fake sometimes.

The biggest problem is that the algorithm hides the very people I need to talk to—the small creators! But my next steps are to finish my expert reading and start finding those first 10 creators on Reddit and creator forums. Thank you."
----------------------------------------
(Simple Talking Points)

- [ ] THE HOOK: Algorithms pick winners and losers.
- [ ] THE PRESSURE: New creators feel they MUST follow trends to be seen.
- [ ] THE IMPACT: Stress and "acting fake" to gain followers.
- [ ] THE QUESTION: How does this pressure change how creators feel and what they post?
- [ ] THE PLAN: Surveys (for numbers) + Interviews (for real stories).
- [ ] THE GOAL: Mapping emotional moments to view count drops.
- [ ] WHY IT MATTERS: Explaining why social media feels unauthentic.
- [ ] THE HURDLE: Finding creators that the algorithm is already hiding.
- [ ] NEXT STEPS: Finishing expert reading and starting recruitment.
